--- author: tetrisd title: D-separation in Bayesian Networks layout: post --- In the topic of Bayesian networks, the concept of d-separation/d-connectedness is introduced to determine quickly conditional independence relations among nodes.
Definition: Let $A$ and $B$ be two nodes with evidence set $\textbf{E}$. An undirected path $(A, n_1, \dots, n_k, B)$ is said to be blocked if and only if the following conditions hold for each node $n_i$ along the path:
  1. $n_i \in \textbf{E}$ and there are directed edges $n_{i-1} \rightarrow n_i \rightarrow n_{i+1}$
  2. $n_i \in \textbf{E}$ and $n_{i-1} \leftarrow n_i \leftarrow n_{i+1}$
  3. $n_i \in \textbf{E}$ and $n_{i-1} \leftarrow n_i \rightarrow n_{i+1}$
  4. $n_i \notin \textbf{E}$ and $n_{i-1} \rightarrow n_i \leftarrow n_{i+1}$

Otherwise, the path is said to be connected. If all undirected paths between $A$ and $B$ are blocked, $A$ and $B$ are d-separated. Otherwise, $A$ and $B$ are d-connected.

Theorem: $A$ and $B$ are conditionally independent given $\textbf{E}$ if and only if they are d-separated.
